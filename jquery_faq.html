Big Bend

  • Contains geological formations from sea fossils and dinosaur bones to volcanic dikes that mar the desert landscape
  • Boasts more types of birds, bats, butterflies, ants, scorpions, and cacti than any other national park in the United States
  • Portions of the Comanche Trail, the same route Comanche warriors once traveled on raids into Mexico, are transitable
  • Established as Texas Canyons State Park in May 1933

Fort Davis

  • One of the best surviving examples of an Indian Wars' frontier military post in the Southwest
  • Active fort from 1854 to 1891
  • The first camels passed by Fort Davis in July 1857 under charge of a naval officer, Lt. Edward F. Beale, who had been assigned to survey a wagon road across Arizona. The lieutenant already had high praise for the efficiency and endurance of the animals.
  • With a pride in the uniform, an individual morale, and a unit espirit surpassed by few regiments, the Buffalo Soldiers of Fort Davis compiled a notable record on the Indian frontier.
